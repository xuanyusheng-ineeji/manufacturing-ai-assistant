# Filling Weight Quality Control SOP

## Purpose

This procedure defines the actions required to monitor and control
product filling weight during production.

## Weight Classification

Each measurement is classified as:

- OK: Weight is between the lower and upper limits.
- UNDER: Weight is below the lower limit.
- OVER: Weight is above the upper limit.

UNDER products must be separated and cannot be released without
additional inspection.

OVER products may cause material loss and must be monitored even when
the product can still be released.

## Start-up Inspection

Before mass production:

1. Confirm the product code.
2. Confirm the standard filling weight.
3. Confirm the lower and upper limits.
4. Confirm the correct equipment setting.
5. Measure at least five test products.
6. Start mass production only when all test results are acceptable.

## Continuous UNDER Results

When three consecutive UNDER results occur:

1. Stop the filling operation.
2. Separate products produced after the previous normal result.
3. Check the filling nozzle for blockage.
4. Check material supply pressure.
5. Check whether the filling setting has changed.
6. Check the weight sensor calibration.
7. Perform five test fillings before restarting production.
8. Record the corrective action.

## Continuous OVER Results

When three consecutive OVER results occur:

1. Check the filling quantity setting.
2. Check whether filling pressure is above the normal range.
3. Check whether the nozzle closing response is delayed.
4. Inspect the product for excessive material.
5. Adjust the filling setting.
6. Perform five test fillings.
7. Record the material loss and corrective action.

## Abnormal Rate Management

The abnormal rate is calculated as:

Abnormal rate = abnormal measurements / total measurements × 100

If the abnormal rate exceeds 5%:

1. Analyze the results by equipment.
2. Analyze the results by product.
3. Separate OVER and UNDER cases.
4. Review whether the abnormal results are concentrated in a
   specific production period.
5. Confirm equipment calibration history.
6. Escalate the issue to the production supervisor.

## Product Changeover

During product changeover:

1. Stop material supply.
2. Remove remaining material.
3. Clean the tank and filling line.
4. Confirm that no previous product remains.
5. Load the new product setting.
6. Confirm the standard weight and limits.
7. Perform test filling.
8. Start production after quality approval.

## Record Management

The following information must be recorded:

- Production order
- Product code
- Equipment code
- Inspection time
- Measured weight
- Result classification
- Operator
- Corrective action
- Calibration result
